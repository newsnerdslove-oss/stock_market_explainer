# Curriculum — basics → advanced, mapped toward Series 7

The platform's content ladder. Levels 1–3 build day-trading literacy; levels 4–5 push toward Series 7 exam coverage. Each module is a row in the `modules` table; lessons and questions hang off it.

> Disclaimer: this teaches the *subject matter* covered by the Series 7 (SIE + Series 7 top-off). It is not affiliated with FINRA and does not certify anyone.

## Level 1 — Market literacy (basics)
- What a market is: exchanges, tickers, bid/ask/spread, market hours.
- Reading price: **line charts vs. candlestick charts**, what a single candle encodes (open/high/low/close), wicks/bodies, green vs. red.
- Order types: market, limit, stop, stop-limit.
- **P&L** explained: realized vs. unrealized, cost basis, fees/slippage.

## Level 2 — Valuation & fundamentals
- **P/E ratio**, EPS, market cap, dividend yield — what they mean and their limits.
- Balance sheet / income statement basics.
- Sectors, indices (S&P 500, Nasdaq), ETFs vs. single stocks.
- Crypto fundamentals: coins vs. tokens, supply, market cap, on-chain vs. exchange price.

## Level 3 — Technical analysis & trader behavior
- Candlestick patterns (doji, hammer, engulfing) — what they suggest and *don't*.
- Indicators: moving averages, RSI, MACD, volume.
- Support/resistance, trends, breakouts.
- **Swing trading vs. day trading**: time horizons, what each type of trader is doing and why; risk per trade, position sizing.

## Level 4 — Products & strategies (Series 7 territory begins)
- **Options**: calls/puts, strike, expiry, premium, intrinsic vs. time value; covered calls, protective puts, spreads — what options traders are doing.
- Margin accounts, leverage, short selling, settlement (T+1).
- Bonds & fixed income: coupon, yield, duration, price/yield inverse.
- Mutual funds, ETFs, packaged products.

## Level 5 — Regulation, suitability & exam mastery (Series 7 core)
- Regulatory framework: SEC, FINRA, registration, the SIE vs. Series 7 structure.
- Customer accounts, suitability, KYC, fiduciary basics.
- Trading rules, prohibited practices, reporting.
- Municipal securities, options rules, margin math at exam depth.
- **Exam mode**: timed mock exams modeled on Series 7 format with scored reports and per-topic weak-spot analysis.

---

## Question bank guidance
- Tag every question with a `series7_tag` (e.g., `options`, `margin`, `suitability`) so exam mode can balance topic weighting like the real exam.
- Always store an `explanation` — the explanation is where learning happens, especially on wrong answers.
- Mix formats: definitions, scenario ("a customer wants X, which is suitable?"), and calculation (margin, breakeven, yield).
- For real Series 7 prep depth, plan to license or write a large bank (the live exam is 125 scored questions). Start with ~20 per module and grow.
